On my mind
Transformative climate justice Sociolinguistics How narratives shape our expectations

Someone once asked me why I do what I do. I told them: I'm not doing anything for self-gratification. There is no monetary goal at all. I simply want to help people. That's it.

After all, what is life without experience?

I have this urge — a very consistent, very persistent urge — to put things in the right order. To build systems. To make things work. But equally, to break them when they stop serving people, and grow beyond them entirely. Growing is one of the few things in life that is entirely self-determined. It has nothing to do with age, or experience, or permission. If you want to grow, just grow.

There is no real-life Superman, for my heroes are my parents. Be strong, be kind, independent, and unstoppable in what you love. One day the ball will stop dribbling and the paintbrush will no longer paint — so while the ball still dribbles, while the paint is still wet, give it everything.
